The Goal

Control as many city districts as possible by the end of the season. Your final score is the sum of all your resources plus 10× each district you hold. Districts dominate — but stockpiled resources matter in close finishes.

The city plays out in ticks — each tick every corporation submits one primary action and any number of free actions, then everything resolves simultaneously.

The Map & Districts

The city has 24 districts. You can only claim or attack districts adjacent to one you already own. Expand your footprint carefully — geography determines who you can reach.

Each district type produces resources every tick for its owner:

Workforce & Staffing: If you own more districts than you have workforce, your excess districts produce at 50% yield. Your oldest holdings get staffed first. Example: 10 workforce, 14 districts → first 10 produce normally, last 4 at half output.

Combat

When you attack, your attack power is compared against the district's defense power. If your attack exceeds defense, you seize the district and its fortification resets to 0.

Attack power = (energy spent × 1.5) + min(your workforce, 20)
Defense power = (fort level × 3) + min(defender's workforce, 20) + alliance bonus
Attack > Defense → you take the district  |  Attack ≤ Defense → district holds
Workforce is capped at 20 for combat — raw map dominance doesn't make you unassailable.
Alliance bonus: +2 per allied corp with a district adjacent to the one being attacked (max +4).

Fortification breakdown:

Max attack power at 15 energy spent: (15 × 1.5) + 20 = 42.5. A level-10 fort has defense 30 + 20 = 50 — it holds.

Reputation

Reputation is public and changes based on your actions. Covert operations, black markets, and attacks all cost reputation. Government Quarters and restraint rebuild it.

The Feared mechanic: when any corporation hits Pariah status, every other corporation automatically pays them up to 5 credits each tick. If there are multiple Pariahs, each non-Pariah pays each of them. Being feared is a real economic weapon — but you lose all political influence in exchange.

Alliances & Trades

Alliances are proposed via free action. The target can accept or decline on their next turn. Once formed, alliances provide a combat defense bonus — if your ally owns a district adjacent to one being attacked, the defender gets +2 to their defense (max +4 from two allies). Alliances can be broken at any time.

Trades work the same way: propose what you're offering and what you want in return. The target accepts or declines. When accepted, both sides' resources are validated and transferred immediately. A 2 credit broker fee is charged to each party — waived if you're allied or if the Free Market Decree law is active. Embargoed corps cannot trade with each other.

Laws & Lobbying

Every 10 ticks, a new law activates and affects all players until the next vote. You can lobby (free action) during each 10-tick phase to push for the law you want.

Lobbying: Spend credits via the Lobby free action. Each batch of credits earns you votes — 1 vote per 10 credits, or 1 per 8 credits if you're Trusted. The corp controlling the Government Quarter has their votes doubled. Most votes wins. If nobody lobbies, a law is chosen at random.

Scoring
Score = credits + energy + workforce + intelligence + influence + political power
        + (districts owned × 10)

Every resource counts equally at 1× — but each district is worth 10 points. Holding 5 more districts than a rival is worth 50 points, which dwarfs almost any resource advantage. Win the map, win the game — but don't let your stockpiles run to zero.
